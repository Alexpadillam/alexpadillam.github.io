About me

My name is Alejandro Padilla, and I’m a 21-year-old graphic design student in my final year, based in León, Guanajuato, Mexico.

While completing my degree, I’ve built a strong foundation in visual communication, 3D modeling, illustration, and fashion design. Through academic and self-initiated projects, from editorial layouts and posters to designing and sewing garment,  I’ve developed a multidisciplinary portfolio that reflects both creative ambition and technical growth.

I’m especially drawn to how design can distort, reflect, or reimagine reality. My work often explores this through bold storytelling and narrative-driven concepts, always aiming to create something thoughtful, original, and worth a closer look.

ABSURD Magazine

Project Overview

ABSURD is a concept magazine that features fictional news stories designed to blur the line between reality and imagination. Each article, photograph, and graphic element is meticulously crafted to create an immersive experience that leans into the surreal.

The design direction intentionally plays with visual tension, juxtaposing traditional magazine layout conventions with disruptive, unexpected elements that mirror the absurd nature of the content.

Project Details

Year: 2024

Tools Used: Blender, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign

Gashadokuro

Project Overview

This illustration is inspired by the Japanese yokai Gashadokuro,a giant skeleton formed from the bones of those who died from starvation. Created for a student-organized exhibition focused on Japanese culture, the piece blends traditional folklore with contemporary techniques.

The process began with a 3D composition reference, which served as the foundation for digital painting. The final artwork merges stylistic elements of classical Japanese art with modern digital aesthetics, resulting in a piece that feels both timeless and current.

Project Details

Year: 2024

Tools Used: Blender, Adobe Photoshop

Alien Sculpt

Project Overview

This alien bust was sculpted in Blender as part of an exploratory series focused on creature design. The aim was to push the boundaries of humanoid anatomy while refining skills in digital sculpting.

Special attention was given to the interplay of organic textures and alien anatomy, creating a form that feels both unfamiliar and oddly believable.

GV Beowulf

Project Overview

GV Beowulf is a concept design for the fourth generation of a smart helmet line. The project blends sleek, futuristic aesthetics with functional technology, imagining a product that could realistically exist in the near future.

The helmet was designed alongside mock advertising materials to simulate a commercial launch, emphasizing both visual appeal and technical innovation.

NAUSEA

Project Overview

NAUSEA is a conceptual poster for a fictional psychological horror film. Drawing on themes of surrealism and body horror, the design uses unsettling imagery and jarring typography to evoke a visceral sense of unease.

The poster aims to disturb and intrigue, pulling the viewer in with a visual language that mirrors the mental unraveling portrayed in the film’s narrative.

Under the Same Stars

Project Overview

Under the Same Stars is a children’s picture book developed as a final university project. The assignment was to create an illustrated book addressing a social issue, and this piece tackles religious conflict through a lens of cultural empathy.

The story encourages children to embrace diversity and understand different belief systems, using gentle illustrations and accessible storytelling to promote acceptance and curiosity.

Ligneous

Project Overview

Ligneous is a 3D sculpting project centered on a hyper-realistic wooden head rendered in Blender. The work explores the convergence of human anatomy and natural materials, emphasizing wood’s organic grain and texture.

The main design challenge was to preserve anatomical realism while convincingly replicating the physical properties of wood, such as grain direction and surface imperfections, across the contours of the face.

Project Details

Year: 2023

Tools Used: Blender, Adobe Photoshop

Justice

Project Overview

This monochrome illustration of the Justice tarot card was created for linocut printing. The design reinterprets the traditional symbolism of the card through a contemporary lens while preserving its core iconography.

Tailored to the linocut medium, the illustration emphasizes bold linework, strong contrast, and strategic use of negative space to maximize visual impact in print.

Baggy Jeans Concept

Project Overview

This project involved designing and producing a pair of baggy jeans entirely from scratch, serving as a personal study in fashion design and garment construction.

Beginning with body measurements, the pattern was created in Clo3D and simulated before being rendered in Blender using custom textures developed in Adobe Photoshop. The design was then brought into the physical world by translating it onto fabric, cutting, and sewing it into a wearable prototype.

Project Details

Year: 2024

Tools Used: Clo3D, Blender, Adobe Photoshop
